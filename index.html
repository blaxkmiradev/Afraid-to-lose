Personal Essay

Afraid
to Lose You

The silent kind of love — the kind that lives between the lines, unnamed and aching.

by rikixz 2025
✦

It started as friendship — and that was the most dangerous part.

They were never supposed to be anything more. Just two people who found each other at the right time, laughing too easily, staying up too late. It felt safe because it was unnamed. And unnamed things have no rules to break.

But somewhere between the shared silences and the texts that came without reason, something quietly shifted. Not in a single moment — not the way movies promise. It was slower than that. Softer. More dangerous. Feelings grew like ivy on old brick — patient, invisible, until suddenly they covered everything.

The Crossing

There's a line between friends and something more. Neither of them said the words when they crossed it. Maybe they didn't notice. Maybe they chose not to. But the crossing happened — in every glance that lasted a beat too long, in every inside joke that became its own private world, in every quiet moment one of them thought: I would do anything not to lose this.

The fear isn't losing a lover. It's losing the friend who somehow became the person you build your ordinary days around — without ever meaning to.

The Weight of Silence

And that's the cruelest part. You love someone who was never yours to lose. There's no breakup to explain the grief. No title to justify the ache. You are just a friend — at least on paper — carrying feelings that have no proper name and no safe place to land.

Every moment feels fragile now. You choose your words carefully. You laugh a little too hard at their jokes. You notice things you shouldn't — the way they tilt their head, the specific way they say your name. And you say nothing, because saying something might shatter the only thing you have left: proximity.

Loving someone silently is its own kind of exhaustion. Caring too much, holding it all in — watching them live their life while you stand just close enough to feel the warmth, but never quite inside it. Beautiful and terrible at the same time, the way only real things can be.

❧

So you stay. You stay as a friend, as the constant, as the one who always shows up. Because the alternative — losing them entirely — is unthinkable. And maybe that's its own kind of love: choosing someone's presence over your own confession. Choosing them, even in silence.

They'll never know how much they mean. Or maybe someday they will. But for now, you hold it the way you hold your breath underwater — careful, tight, hoping you surface before you run out.

This one is for everyone who loves someone who was never truly theirs — and yet, somehow, always was. ;)
